Getting started


Step 1: Create an account

Congrats, you already completed step 1 by creating an account


Step 2: Complete your profile

In order to take advantage of the portfolio builder function, you must first complete your profile with a few simple details. The first profile variable is your investment time horizon, or the amount of time you expect to hold onto your investments before selling. This can also mean that part of your portfolio is going to be held in cash if your investment horizon is very short.

The other variable of your profile is risk tolerance. This is simply a measure of how comfortable you are with investment risk and potential losses. For example, stocks are more risky than bonds, but also have more potential upside return on investment. So, in general, people with a higher risk tolerance will have more of their portfolio in stocks because that provides greater chance of high returns, but at the risk of more dramatic losses.

You can change both of these settings in the profile tab, and if you are unsure about your level of risk tolerance there is a brief survey which will assign you a level of risk tolerance based on your responses.

Step 3: Look at your portfolio

As soon as you have completed your profile (and any time afterwards you update it) a personalized portfolio will be automatically built for you. You can view this portfolio by selecting it on the Portfolio List tab.

Step 4 (optional): Create a customized portfolio

Click on the "Create Customized Portfolio" tab and you will be able to make your own portfolio with whatever asset allocations you choose. This portfolio will be added to you list and can be viewed, edited, or deleted.

Step 5: Simulate your portfolio

From the page where you view your potfolio, you will be able to either view a previous simulation or run a new simulation. You will be able to modify all the input variables for the simulation besides the asset allocation of the portfolio. The default values for the simulation are based on historical averages, and will be automatically used unless you specify a different value.

Example: you run a simulation for your portfolio that is comprised of 50% stocks and 50% bonds. You modify the average yearly return and standard deviation for stocks, but you do not change any of the values for bonds or inflation. The simulation will run with your modified stock return and standard deviation numbers while all other calculations will be done using historical averages.

Step 6: Look at the simulation results dashboard

Once your simulation is run, you will automatically be taken to the dashboard page where you can view the results of your simulation. These results are generated using a simple Monte Carlo method and what you see are is the arithmatic mean (both nominal and real) returns of your portfolio.
