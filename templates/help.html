Getting started


Step 1: Create an account

Congrats, you already completed step 1 by creating an account


Step 2: Complete your profile

In order to take advantage of the portfolio builder function, you must first complete your profile with a few simple details. The first profile variable is your investment time horizon, or the amount of time you expect to hold onto your investments before selling. This can also mean that part of your portfolio is going to be held in cash if your investment horizon is very short.

The other variable of your profile is risk tolerance. This is simply a measure of how comfortable you are with investment risk and potential losses. For example, stocks are more risky than bonds, but also have more potential upside return on investment. So, in general, people with a higher risk tolerance will have more of their portfolio in stocks because that provides greater chance of high returns, but at the risk of more dramatic losses.

You can change both of these settings in the profile tab, and if you are unsure about your level of risk tolerance there is a brief survey which will assign you a level of risk tolerance based on your responses.

Step 3: Look at your portfolio

As soon as you have completed your profile (and any time afterwards you update it) a personalized portfolio will be automatically built for you. You can view this portfolio by selecting it on the Portfolio List tab.
